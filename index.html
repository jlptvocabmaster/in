🎌 What is the JLPT?
The Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) — 日本語能力試験 — is the world's most recognised Japanese certification, taken by over 1 million people each year in 90+ countries. It has five levels: N5 (easiest) → N1 (hardest). Passing opens doors to Japanese universities, workplaces, and visa applications.
✍️ Japan's Three Writing Scripts
Japanese uses three scripts together in the same sentence:
あ ア
Kana
Phonetic syllables (hiragana + katakana). Learnt first.
漢
Kanji
Chinese-origin logographs. Each has meaning & reading.
ABC
Rōmaji
Latin letters — used to transliterate Japanese for learners.
Furigana (small kana above kanji) help you read unfamiliar characters — this app shows furigana on every word so you can build reading confidence from day one.
🖊️ What is Kanji?
Kanji are logographic characters borrowed from Chinese. Each carries a meaning and one or more readings. For example: 水 (mizu / sui) means "water". The JLPT tests: N5 ~100 kanji · N4 ~300 · N3 ~650 · N2 ~1,000 · N1 ~2,000. Recognising kanji is the single biggest key to reading Japanese fluently.
📚 Vocabulary (語彙 — Goi)
Vocabulary is tested in two ways on the JLPT: choosing the correct meaning of a word, and choosing the correct reading (furigana). This app trains both. The number of words per level roughly doubles as you advance:

💡 How to Use This App
1. Pick your level above (start at N5 if you're new).
2. Choose a mode — Meaning quiz, Reading quiz, Type It (romaji input), Flashcard flip, or authentic Past Paper questions.
3. Set session size — 10 words is great for a quick study break; go All for a full drill.
4. Earn XP & track streaks — mistakes are saved to your Mistake Bank for focused review.
5. Daily Word — a new word every day keeps you on a study streak 🔥.

Registration fills fast — apply early!
